
### 3.10.3 Infographics

**Why Infographics Work for SEO:**

- 3x more likely to be shared than any other content
- Increase website traffic by 12% on average
- Excellent for backlinks (people reference and link)
- Improve time on page
- Visual content processed 60,000x faster than text
- Great for featured snippets and image search

```markdown
3. Process Infographics
   - Step-by-step guides

   - Workflows

```

**Infographic SEO Best Practices:**

**1. Design Specifications**

```markdown
**Dimensions:**
Optimal: 800 × 2000-8000 pixels (portrait)
Width: 600-1000 pixels (mobile-friendly)
Height: No limit, but aim for scrollable (not too long)

**File Format:**
✅ PNG: Best for infographics (lossless, supports transparency)
✅ JPG: Good for photo-heavy graphics (smaller file size)
❌ GIF: Avoid (limited colors, larger file size)
✅ SVG: Excellent for simple vector graphics (scalable)

**File Size:**
Target: Under 1.5MB
Maximum: 5MB
Tools to compress:
- TinyPNG
- Compressor.io
- ImageOptim (Mac)

**Colors:**
- High contrast for readability
- Consistent color palette (3-5 colors max)
- Accessible contrast ratios (WCAG AA)
- Brand colors for recognition

**Fonts:**
- Readable from thumbnail size
- Minimum: 14pt for body text
- Headlines: 24-36pt
- Maximum: 2-3 font families
- Ensure web-safe fonts or embedded fonts

**Branding:**
✅ Logo in corner or footer
✅ Website URL prominently displayed
✅ Consistent with brand guidelines
✅ Contact info (for large campaigns)
```

**2. Content Structure**

```markdown
**The 60-30-10 Rule:**

60% - Visual elements:
- Icons, illustrations, charts
- Graphics representing data
- Visual hierarchy elements

30% - White space:
- Breathing room between sections
- Prevents overwhelming viewers
- Improves comprehension

10% - Text:
- Concise headlines
- Key statistics
- Minimal body copy
- Source citations

**Information Flow:**

Top (Header):
- Attention-grabbing title
- Subtitle or value proposition
- Your logo/brand

Middle (Body):
- Main content in logical sections
- Clear numbering or steps
- Visual breaks between sections
- Icons to illustrate points

Bottom (Footer):
- Call-to-action
- Website URL (large, readable)
- Social media handles
- Data sources
- Copyright/year
```

**5. Promoting Infographics for Backlinks**

```markdown
**Outreach Email Template:**

Subject: Quick question about [Their Article Topic]

Hi [Name],

I recently came across your article "[Article Title]" and 
loved your take on [specific point].

I noticed you mentioned [topic related to your infographic]. 
I just created a visual guide that breaks down [topic] with 
the latest statistics and data.

[Link to infographic]

If you think it would add value to your readers, feel free 
to reference it or embed it in your article. Either way, 
keep up the great work!

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. If you do decide to include it, just let me know – 
I'd love to share your article with my audience too.

**Follow-up (7 days later):**

Hi [Name],

Following up on my previous email about the [topic] 
infographic. I know you're busy, so no worries if it's 
not a fit!

Just wanted to make sure you saw it, as I think your 
readers would find it helpful given your article on 
[specific topic].

Thanks for your time!
[Your Name]

**Outreach Best Practices:**

✅ Personalize each email
✅ Reference specific content on their site
✅ Explain clear value for their audience
✅ Don't be pushy about links
✅ Offer to reciprocate
✅ Follow up once (not multiple times)
✅ Keep it brief (under 150 words)
```

**6. Tracking Infographic Performance**

```markdown
**Metrics to Monitor:**

Shares:
- Twitter shares (via Twitter Analytics)
- Facebook shares (via Facebook Insights)
- Pinterest pins (via Pinterest Analytics)
- LinkedIn shares

Embeds:
- Number of sites embedding
- Quality of embedding sites
- Traffic from embeds

Backlinks:
- New backlinks acquired
- Domain authority of linking sites
- Anchor text used
- Link placement (content vs footer/sidebar)

Traffic:
- Direct visits to infographic page
- Referral traffic from embeds
- Time on page
- Bounce rate

Conversions:
- Email signups from infographic page
- Downloads (if gated)
- Contact form submissions
- Newsletter subscriptions

**Tools:**
- Google Analytics (traffic, conversions)
- Ahrefs/SEMrush (backlinks)
- BuzzSumo (social shares)
- Google Search Console (search performance)
```
