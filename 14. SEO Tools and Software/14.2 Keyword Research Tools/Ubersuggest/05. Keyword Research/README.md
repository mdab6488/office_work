
## Keyword Research

### How to Perform Keyword Research

#### Step 1: Enter Your Seed Keyword
```
Example: "coffee machines"
```

#### Step 2: Select Location and Language
- Choose your target country (e.g., United States)
- Select language (e.g., English)

#### Step 3: Analyze Results

The tool provides:
- **Search Volume**: Monthly average searches
- **SEO Difficulty (SD)**: 0-100 scale (higher = harder to rank)
- **Paid Difficulty (PD)**: Competition for paid ads
- **Cost Per Click (CPC)**: Average ad cost

### Real-Life Example 1: E-commerce Store

**Scenario**: You run an online store selling coffee equipment.

**Process**:
1. Enter "coffee grinder" in Ubersuggest
2. Results show:
   - Search Volume: 74,000/month
   - SEO Difficulty: 58 (Medium)
   - CPC: $1.45

3. Click "View All Keyword Ideas" to see:
   - "best coffee grinder" - 33,100 searches, SD: 67
   - "burr coffee grinder" - 22,200 searches, SD: 52
   - "manual coffee grinder" - 14,800 searches, SD: 49
   - "coffee grinder reviews" - 8,100 searches, SD: 63

**Action**: Target "manual coffee grinder" (lower competition, decent volume) for a product category page.

### Keyword Filters

Use filters to refine results:
- **Search Volume**: Min/Max monthly searches
- **SEO Difficulty**: Find easier keywords (0-40)
- **Paid Difficulty**: Identify low-competition PPC opportunities
- **Word Count**: Long-tail keywords (3+ words)

### Real-Life Example 2: Blog Content

**Scenario**: Creating a fitness blog.

**Process**:
1. Search "home workout"
2. Filter: SD < 30, Search Volume > 1,000
3. Found keywords:
   - "home workout no equipment" - 12,100, SD: 28
   - "home workout plan" - 9,900, SD: 27
   - "home workout for beginners" - 8,100, SD: 25

**Action**: Create comprehensive guides targeting these lower-competition keywords.
