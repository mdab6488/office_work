
## Core Functions

### Function 1: Discover New Keywords

#### How It Works

1. **Click "Discover new keywords"**

2. **Choose your starting point:**

   **A. Start with keywords**
   - Enter words or phrases (up to 10 at a time)
   - Example: "running shoes", "marathon training", "trail running"
   
   **B. Start with a website**
   - Enter your website or a competitor's
   - Google analyzes the site and suggests relevant keywords
   - Example: www.nike.com/running

3. **Set your targeting:**
   - **Location**: Where your customers are
   - **Language**: What language they speak
   - **Search networks**: Google + search partners

4. **Click "Get Results"**

#### Understanding the Results

You'll see a table with columns:

| Column | What It Means | Example |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Keyword** | The search term | "best running shoes" |
| **Avg. Monthly Searches** | Average searches per month (last 12 months) | 90,500 |
| **Competition** | How many advertisers compete for this keyword | Low / Medium / High |
| **Competition (indexed value)** | Numerical competition score (0-100) | 67 |
| **Top of Page Bid (low range)** | Minimum suggested bid for top position | $1.50 |
| **Top of Page Bid (high range)** | Maximum suggested bid for top position | $4.20 |

### Function 2: Get Search Volume and Forecasts

#### How It Works

1. **Click "Get search volume and forecasts"**

2. **Input your keywords:**
   - Paste a list (one per line or comma-separated)
   - Upload a CSV file
   - Example list:
     ```
     running shoes
     nike running shoes
     best running shoes for beginners
     marathon training shoes
     trail running shoes
     ```

3. **Set targeting** (location, language, etc.)

4. **View two tabs:**

   **Historical Metrics**
   - Past search volume data
   - Trends over time
   - Competition levels
   
   **Forecasts**
   - Predicted clicks
   - Estimated impressions
   - Projected costs (for paid ads)
   - Based on your budget and bid settings
