## 3.2 Keyword Optimization

### Keyword Placement

**What It Is:**
Strategically positioning your target keywords in specific locations on your page where Google pays the most attention.

**Why It Matters:**
Where you place keywords signals to Google what your page is about. Strategic placement improves relevance without keyword stuffing.

**Critical Keyword Placement Locations (Priority Order):**

#### 1. Title Tag (Most Important)
**Rules:**
- Include primary keyword near the beginning
- Keep under 60 characters (to avoid truncation)
- Make it compelling for clicks

❌ **Poor Title Tag:**
```
"Everything You Need to Know About Gardening and Growing Plants"
- Generic
- Keyword too far from beginning
- Not specific
```

✅ **Optimized Title Tag:**
```
"How to Grow Tomatoes: Beginner's Guide (8 Simple Steps)"
- Primary keyword ("grow tomatoes") at the start
- Compelling (beginner-friendly, numbered)
- 55 characters
```

#### 2. URL Slug
**Rules:**
- Short and descriptive
- Include primary keyword
- Use hyphens, not underscores
- Lowercase only

❌ **Poor URL:**
```
website.com/blog/post-12345-about-growing-tomatoes-in-your-garden
```

✅ **Optimized URL:**
```
website.com/how-to-grow-tomatoes
```

#### 3. H1 Heading (Page Title)
**Rules:**
- Only one H1 per page
- Should match or closely relate to title tag
- Include primary keyword naturally

❌ **Poor H1:**
```
"Welcome to My Gardening Blog"
```

✅ **Optimized H1:**
```
"How to Grow Tomatoes: Complete Beginner's Guide"
```

#### 4. First 100-150 Words (Introduction)
**Rules:**
- Mention primary keyword in first paragraph
- Sounds natural, not forced
- Answers search intent immediately

✅ **Example:**
```
Growing tomatoes in your home garden is easier than you think. Whether 
you're planting in containers on your balcony or in raised beds, this 
guide will walk you through exactly how to grow tomatoes from seed to 
harvest. By following these 8 steps, you'll be enjoying fresh, homegrown 
tomatoes in about 60-85 days.
```

#### 5. Subheadings (H2, H3)
**Rules:**
- Include primary or related keywords naturally
- Use variations, not exact repeats
- Make headers descriptive

✅ **Example Structure:**
```
H1: How to Grow Tomatoes: Complete Beginner's Guide

H2: When to Plant Tomatoes (By Climate Zone)
H2: Choosing the Best Tomato Varieties for Your Garden
H2: Preparing Soil for Tomato Plants
H3: Ideal Soil pH for Tomatoes
H3: Essential Nutrients for Tomato Growth
H2: Watering Tomatoes: How Much and How Often
H2: Common Tomato Growing Problems (And Solutions)
```

#### 6. Image Alt Text
**Rules:**
- Describe what's in the image
- Include keyword when relevant
- Keep under 125 characters
- Don't keyword stuff

❌ **Poor Alt Text:**
```
alt="tomatoes tomatoes grow tomatoes growing tomatoes"
```

✅ **Good Alt Text:**
```
alt="Hands planting tomato seedling in garden soil"
alt="Ripe red tomatoes growing on vine in backyard garden"
```

#### 7. Meta Description
**Rules:**
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Compelling call-to-action
- Not a direct ranking factor, but affects click-through rate

✅ **Example:**
```
"Learn how to grow tomatoes in 8 easy steps. This beginner-friendly 
guide covers planting, watering, soil prep, and common problems. 
Start your tomato garden today!"
```

#### 8. Throughout Body Content
**Rules:**
- Use keyword naturally 3-6 times per 1,000 words
- Focus on variations and synonyms
- Prioritize readability over keyword density

**Complete Placement Example:**

```
URL: yoursite.com/how-to-grow-tomatoes

Title Tag: How to Grow Tomatoes: 8 Steps for Beginners (2025 Guide)

H1: How to Grow Tomatoes: Complete Beginner's Guide

First Paragraph:
"Growing tomatoes is one of the most rewarding experiences for beginner 
gardeners. Whether you want to plant tomatoes in containers or garden 
beds, this guide will show you exactly how to grow tomatoes successfully..."

[Image with alt text: "Person planting tomato seedlings in raised garden bed"]

H2: When to Plant Tomatoes

Body text mentioning "tomato plants," "growing tomatoes," and variations...

Meta Description: Learn how to grow tomatoes with this simple 8-step guide 
for beginners. Covers planting, soil, watering & harvesting. Start growing 
today!
```

### Keyword Density (Natural Usage)

**What It Is:**
The percentage of times your target keyword appears compared to total word count. Modern SEO focuses on natural usage rather than hitting a specific percentage.

**The Old Way (2010s):**
SEOs aimed for 2-3% keyword density, leading to awkward, over-optimized content.

**The Modern Approach (2025):**
Use keywords naturally. Focus on topic coverage and user experience.

**Real-Life Example:**

**Keyword:** "best running shoes"  
**Article Length:** 2,000 words

❌ **Keyword Stuffing (5% density = 100 mentions):**
```
"The best running shoes are essential. When looking for the best running 
shoes, you need the best running shoes that fit properly. We tested the 
best running shoes to find the best running shoes for runners. These 
best running shoes are the best running shoes available. The best running 
shoes will make your runs better..."

[Google penalty likely - unnatural, poor user experience]
```

✅ **Natural Usage (0.8% density = 16 mentions):**
```
"Finding the right running shoes can transform your training. We spent 
500 miles testing top-rated models to identify the best options for 
different foot types and running styles.

In this guide, you'll discover:
- Top picks for neutral, overpronation, and supination
- How to choose the right shoe for your gait
- Price-to-performance analysis
- When to replace your trainers

Our testing process involved 10 runners trying each model on various 
terrains over 8 weeks..."

[Keywords appear naturally where they make sense]
```

**Modern Keyword Usage Guidelines:**

| Article Length | Natural Keyword Mentions |
|----------------|-------------------------|
| 500 words | 2-4 times |
| 1,000 words | 4-8 times |
| 2,000 words | 8-15 times |
| 3,000+ words | 12-20 times |

**What to Do Instead of Keyword Stuffing:**
1. Use synonyms and variations
2. Focus on semantic richness
3. Cover the topic comprehensively
4. Let keywords appear naturally
5. Prioritize readability

**Real-World Impact:**
A website reduced keyword density from 4% to 1% while improving content quality. Results:
- Rankings improved for 67% of target keywords
- Average position increased from 8.3 to 4.7
- Bounce rate decreased 23%
- Time on page increased 41%

### LSI Keywords and Semantic SEO

**What It Is:**
LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) keywords are terms and phrases related to your main keyword. Semantic SEO means Google understands topics conceptually, not just by exact keyword matches.

**Why It Matters:**
Google's algorithm (especially after BERT and RankBrain updates) understands context. Including related terms proves you're covering a topic comprehensively.

**Real-Life Example:**

**Primary Keyword:** "best coffee maker"

**Related LSI Keywords Google Expects:**
- Brewing methods (drip, espresso, French press)
- Features (programmable, thermal carafe, grinder)
- Brands (Keurig, Ninja, Breville, Cuisinart)
- Capacity (single-serve, 12-cup, commercial)
- Price ranges
- Materials (stainless steel, glass carafe)
- Maintenance (descaling, filter replacement)
- Coffee terms (bloom, extraction, temperature)

❌ **Without LSI Keywords (Thin Content):**
```
"The best coffee maker makes great coffee. We reviewed coffee makers 
to find the best coffee maker. The XYZ coffee maker is the best coffee 
maker. Buy this coffee maker for the best coffee."

[200 words total, just repeating "coffee maker"]
```

✅ **With LSI Keywords (Comprehensive):**
```
"The best coffee maker depends on your brewing preferences and lifestyle. 
Whether you're looking for a single-serve Keurig for convenience, a 
programmable drip machine for your morning routine, or an espresso 
maker for café-quality lattes, this guide has you covered.

We tested 15 models over 6 months, evaluating:
- Brew quality and temperature consistency (195-205°F ideal range)
- Ease of use (programmable timers, auto-shutoff features)
- Build quality (stainless steel vs plastic components)
- Capacity options (single-serve to 14-cup thermal carafe)
- Maintenance requirements (self-cleaning cycles, descaling)

Top Picks by Category:

Best Overall: Breville Precision Brewer ($300)
- SCA-certified brewing temperature
- Multiple brewing modes (Gold, Fast, Strong, Iced, Cold Brew)
- Adjustable bloom time for optimal extraction
- Thermal carafe retains heat for 2+ hours
- Built-in grinder compatibility

Best Budget: Black+Decker 12-Cup ($25)
- Basic programmable timer
- Auto-pause feature for mid-brew pouring
- Glass carafe with warming plate
- 2-hour auto-shutoff for safety..."

[Continues with detailed reviews, comparisons, buying guide]
```

**How to Find LSI Keywords:**

1. **Google's "People Also Ask" Section**
```
Search: "coffee maker"
Google shows:
- "What is the best type of coffee maker?"
- "How long should a coffee maker last?"
- "Is it worth getting an expensive coffee maker?"
- "What coffee maker does Starbucks use?"
```

2. **Google's Related Searches (Bottom of SERP)**
```
- best coffee maker with grinder
- commercial coffee maker
- coffee maker reviews consumer reports
- programmable coffee maker
- thermal carafe coffee maker
```

3. **Google Autocomplete**
```
Type: "coffee maker..."
Suggests:
- coffee maker with grinder
- coffee maker for office
- coffee maker brands
- coffee maker walmart
```

4. **Top-Ranking Competitor Content**
Analyze what terms competitors ranking in top 3 positions use throughout their content.

5. **Tools:**
- LSIGraph.com
- Surfer SEO
- Clearscope
- SEMrush's SEO Content Template

**Practical Implementation:**

Create a "semantic content brief" before writing:

```
Primary Keyword: "best coffee maker"

Must-Include Terms (LSI Keywords):
□ Brewing methods: drip, espresso, pour-over, French press, cold brew
□ Features: programmable, thermal carafe, auto-shutoff, brew strength
□ Brands: Breville, Technivorm, Ninja, Cuisinart, Keurig, Mr. Coffee
□ Capacity: single-serve, 10-cup, 12-cup, 14-cup
□ Materials: stainless steel, glass carafe, BPA-free plastic
□ Maintenance: descaling, cleaning cycle, filter replacement
□ Price: budget, mid-range, premium, under $50, over $200
□ Coffee concepts: extraction, bloom time, temperature, grind size

Subtopics to Cover:
□ Types of coffee makers comparison
□ How to choose based on needs
□ Brewing temperature importance
□ Maintenance and cleaning
□ Value for money analysis
□ Long-term durability
```

**Real-World Impact:**
A product review site rewrote their coffee maker article, increasing LSI keyword usage from 12 to 65 related terms. Results:
- Organic traffic: +215% in 3 months
- Keyword rankings: Now ranking for 83 related keywords vs 12 before
- Featured snippet captured for "how to choose a coffee maker"
- Average position improved from 11.4 to 3.8

### Primary vs Secondary Keywords

**What It Is:**
- **Primary Keyword:** Your main target keyword for the page (typically has highest search volume)
- **Secondary Keywords:** Related keywords you can also rank for with the same piece of content

**Why It Matters:**
A well-optimized page can rank for dozens or even hundreds of keywords. Secondary keywords expand your reach without requiring separate articles.

**Real-Life Example:**

**Topic:** Article about home workouts

**Primary Keyword:**
- "home workout routine" (5,400 monthly searches, difficulty: medium)

**Secondary Keywords:**
- "at home workout plan" (2,900 searches)
- "home exercises without equipment" (1,600 searches)
- "beginner home workout" (1,300 searches)
- "home workout for weight loss" (880 searches)
- "30 minute home workout" (720 searches)
- "full body workout at home" (590 searches)

**How to Structure Content for Multiple Keywords:**

✅ **Strategic Content Structure:**

```
URL: yoursite.com/home-workout-routine
Title: Home Workout Routine: 30-Minute Plan for Beginners (No Equipment)
H1: Complete Home Workout Routine for Beginners

Introduction (targets primary + main secondaries):
"This home workout routine requires no equipment and takes just 30 minutes. 
Whether you're looking for a full body workout at home or a beginner-friendly 
plan for weight loss, this guide has everything you need to start exercising 
at home today."

H2: Benefits of Home Workouts (supports all keywords naturally)

H2: Complete 30-Minute Home Workout Plan (targets: "30 minute home workout")
[Detailed workout with exercises]

H2: Home Workout Routine for Beginners (targets: "beginner home workout")
[Modified versions, progression tips]

H2: Home Exercises Without Equipment (targets: "home exercises without equipment")
[List of bodyweight exercises with descriptions]

H2: Best Home Workout for Weight Loss (targets: "home workout for weight loss")
[Cardio-focused variations, nutrition tips]

H2: Creating Your At-Home Workout Plan (targets: "at home workout plan")
[Weekly schedule, progression strategy]
```

**Keyword Distribution Strategy:**

| Element | Primary Keyword | Secondary Keywords |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| Title Tag | ✓ Yes (early) | ✓ 1-2 variations |
| H1 | ✓ Yes | Maybe 1 |
| URL | ✓ Yes | No |
| First Paragraph | ✓ Yes (within 50 words) | ✓ Mention 2-3 |
| H2 Subheadings | ✓ 1-2 times | ✓ Each H2 targets different secondary |
| Body Content | ✓ Natural usage | ✓ Sprinkle throughout |
| Meta Description | ✓ Yes | ✓ 1-2 if space allows |

**Real Case Study:**

**Before (Single Keyword Focus):**
```
Article: "Home Workout Routine"
- Optimized only for primary keyword
- Rankings: 1 keyword in top 10
- Monthly traffic: 180 visits
```

**After (Primary + Secondary Strategy):**
```
Same Article: Reoptimized for primary + 8 secondary keywords
- Rankings: 12 keywords in top 10
- Monthly traffic: 1,340 visits
- Increase: 644%
```

**How to Find Secondary Keywords:**

1. **Use Keyword Research Tools:**
```
Enter primary keyword in Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Ubersuggest
Filter for:
- Similar search intent
- Related to main topic
- Lower difficulty score
- Decent search volume (100+ searches/month)
```

2. **Analyze Top-Ranking Competitors:**
```
Look at what other keywords top 3 results rank for
Use SEMrush or Ahrefs "Organic Keywords" report
Example: #1 result for "home workout routine" also ranks for 127 other keywords
```

3. **Google Search Console:**
```
Check what keywords your existing pages already rank for (positions 11-30)
These are opportunities to optimize and capture with same content
```

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**

❌ **Mistake 1: Targeting Conflicting Intent**
```
Primary: "buy running shoes" (transactional)
Secondary: "how to tie running shoes" (informational)
→ These need separate pages
```

❌ **Mistake 2: Keyword Cannibalization Risk**
```
Article 1: "home workout routine"
Article 2: "at home workout plan"
→ Too similar; choose one article for both
```

✅ **Correct Approach: Group by Intent**
```
One article can target:
- home workout routine
- at home workout plan  
- home workout for beginners
- 30 minute home workout

(All have same intent: User wants a workout plan)
```

### Long-Tail Keywords

**What It Is:**
Longer, more specific keyword phrases (usually 3-5+ words) with lower search volume but higher intent and conversion rates.

**Why They Matter:**
- Less competition (easier to rank)
- Higher conversion rates (more specific intent)
- Collectively drive substantial traffic
- Voice search is increasing long-tail queries

**The Keyword Spectrum:**

| Type | Example | Monthly Searches | Difficulty | Conversion Rate |
|------|---------|-----------------|------------|----------------|
| Head Term | "shoes" | 550,000 | Very High | 0.5% |
| Body Keyword | "running shoes" | 165,000 | High | 1.2% |
| Long-Tail | "best running shoes for flat feet" | 2,400 | Medium | 3.8% |
| Super Long-Tail | "best running shoes for flat feet under $100" | 320 | Low | 6.2% |

**Real-Life Example:**

**Scenario:** You run a fitness blog and want to rank for "weight loss"

❌ **Poor Strategy (Targeting Head Terms Only):**
```
Target: "weight loss" (368,000 searches/month)
- Extremely high competition
- Competing against WebMD, Healthline, Mayo Clinic
- Vague intent (lose weight, weight loss calculator, weight loss medication?)
- Nearly impossible to rank without massive authority
```

✅ **Smart Strategy (Long-Tail Focus):**
```
Instead, create multiple articles targeting long-tail variations:

1. "how to lose weight after pregnancy" 
   - 2,900 searches, medium difficulty
   - Specific audience with clear intent

2. "weight loss meal plan for vegetarians"
   - 1,600 searches, low-medium difficulty
   - High commercial intent

3. "how much weight can you lose in a month safely"
   - 1,300 searches, medium difficulty
   - Specific question, easy to answer definitively

4. "weight loss plateau what to do"
   - 880 searches, low difficulty
   - Problem-focused, loyal audience

5. "best weight loss apps for iPhone free"
   - 720 searches, low difficulty
   - High conversion potential

6. "how to lose weight without counting calories"
   - 590 searches, low difficulty
   - Popular angle, specific method

Collective Impact:
- Combined: 8,090 monthly searches
- Much easier to rank for all 6
- More engaged, targeted traffic
- Better conversion rates
```

#### 4. Buyer Intent Long-Tail

### Keyword Cannibalization

**What It Is:**
When multiple pages on your website target the same or very similar keywords, causing them to compete against each other in search results. This confuses Google and dilutes your ranking potential.

**Why It's Bad:**
- Pages compete with each other instead of dominating together
- Google may rank the "wrong" page (less optimized or older)
- Your authority is split across multiple pages
- Lower click-through rates overall
- Wasted crawl budget

**Real-Life Example:**

❌ **Cannibalization Problem:**

A fitness website has these pages:
```
Page 1: yoursite.com/home-workout-routine
Title: "Home Workout Routine for Beginners"
Content: General home workout guide

Page 2: yoursite.com/at-home-exercise-plan  
Title: "At-Home Exercise Plan for Beginners"
Content: Very similar home workout information

Page 3: yoursite.com/beginner-home-workouts
Title: "Beginner Home Workouts to Get Started"
Content: Nearly identical beginner home workout advice

Result:
- All 3 pages rank positions 15-30 for "home workout routine"
- None break into top 10
- Google is confused about which page is the authority
- Combined traffic: 200 visits/month
```

✅ **After Fixing Cannibalization:**

```
Strategy: Consolidate into one authoritative page

Keep: yoursite.com/home-workout-routine
- Merged best content from all 3 pages
- Added 1,500 more words of unique content
- Comprehensive guide covering all beginner aspects
- 301 redirects from Page 2 and Page 3 to Page 1

Result after 8 weeks:
- Single page ranks position #3 for "home workout routine"
- Also ranks for 47 related keywords
- Traffic: 1,850 visits/month
- Increase: 825%
```

**How to Identify Keyword Cannibalization:**

**Method 1: Google Search Operator**
```
site:yourwebsite.com "target keyword"

Example: site:yourfitnessblog.com "home workout"

If you see 5+ pages from your site, you likely have cannibalization
```

**Method 2: Google Search Console**
```
1. Go to Performance report
2. Click on a keyword
3. Scroll to "Pages" tab
4. If 2+ pages are ranking for same keyword = cannibalization

Example:
Keyword: "best running shoes"
Page 1: /running-shoes-guide (avg position 12)
Page 2: /best-running-shoes-2025 (avg position 15)
Page 3: /running-shoe-reviews (avg position 23)
→ All competing, none winning
```

**Method 3: Use SEO Tools**
```
Tools with cannibalization reports:
- Ahrefs: Site Audit → Duplicate Content
- SEMrush: Position Tracking → Cannibalization tab
- Sitebulb: Crawler with cannibalization detection
```

**Types of Keyword Cannibalization:**

#### Type 1: Identical Target Keywords
```
❌ Problem:
- Page A: "best coffee makers"
- Page B: "best coffee makers 2025"  
- Page C: "top coffee makers"

✅ Solution: Merge into one ultimate guide
→ "Best Coffee Makers: 2025 Reviews & Buying Guide"
```

#### Type 2: Conflicting Intent
```
❌ Problem:
- Page A: "running shoes" (informational guide)
- Page B: "running shoes" (product category page)

✅ Solution: Differentiate clearly
→ Page A: "How to Choose Running Shoes: Complete Guide"
→ Page B: "Buy Running Shoes - Shop All Brands"
```

#### Type 3: Outdated Duplicate Content
```
❌ Problem:
- /best-smartphones-2022
- /best-smartphones-2023
- /best-smartphones-2024
- /best-smartphones-2025

✅ Solution: Keep only current, redirect old
→ Keep: /best-smartphones (update annually)
→ 301 redirect all old year-specific pages to current
```

#### Type 4: Thin Content Pages
```
❌ Problem:
- /blue-widgets (150 words)
- /best-blue-widgets (200 words)
- /blue-widgets-guide (180 words)
- /buying-blue-widgets (160 words)

✅ Solution: Create one comprehensive resource
→ /blue-widgets-complete-guide (2,500 words covering all aspects)
→ Delete or 301 redirect thin pages
```

**How to Fix Keyword Cannibalization:**

**Solution 1: Merge and Redirect (Most Common)**
```
Best for: Pages with similar content and intent

Steps:
1. Identify which page performs better (traffic, backlinks, rankings)
2. Merge content from weaker pages into stronger page
3. Improve the surviving page (add more value, update)
4. Set up 301 redirects from old URLs to new URL
5. Update internal links

Example:
Keep: /complete-guide-to-email-marketing (best metrics)
Merge: /email-marketing-tips + /email-marketing-basics
Redirect: Both old URLs → /complete-guide-to-email-marketing
```

**Solution 2: De-Optimize and Differentiate**
```
Best for: When both pages have value but target too similar keywords

Steps:
1. Choose primary page for main keyword
2. Modify secondary page to target different angle/keyword
3. Update title tags, H1s, and content focus
4. Add canonical tag if needed

Example:
Page 1: "Best Project Management Software" (comparison focus)
└─ Primary for: "best project management software"

Page 2: "How to Choose Project Management Software"  
└─ Reoptimized for: "how to choose project management software"
└─ Different intent: education vs comparison
```

**Solution 3: Canonicalization**
```
Best for: Similar pages that need to exist separately (e.g., sorting/filter pages)

Steps:
1. Choose the main page
2. Add canonical tag to duplicate/similar pages pointing to main
3. This tells Google which version to rank

Example:
Main: /blue-running-shoes
Variations: 
- /blue-running-shoes?sort=price (add canonical to main)
- /blue-running-shoes?filter=size-10 (add canonical to main)
```

**Solution 4: Delete and 301 Redirect**
```
Best for: Low-value pages that don't deserve to exist

Steps:
1. Identify pages with little traffic or thin content
2. Check for any valuable backlinks (preserve with redirect)
3. Delete the page
4. 301 redirect to most relevant existing page

Example:
Delete: /random-blog-post-about-shoes (10 visits/month, thin)
Redirect: → /ultimate-shoe-buying-guide
```

**Solution 5: Create a Hub-and-Spoke Structure**
```
Best for: Multiple pages that could support a topic cluster

Transform cannibalization into strategic internal linking:

Hub (Pillar): /complete-social-media-marketing-guide
├─ Spoke 1: /instagram-marketing-strategies
```

**Case Study: Real Cannibalization Fix**

**Before:**
```
E-commerce site with:
- 15 blog posts about "leather wallets"
- All ranking positions 20-50
- Combined traffic: 340 visits/month
```

**Analysis:**
```
Posts competing:
1. "Best Leather Wallets for Men"
2. "Top 10 Leather Wallets"
3. "Leather Wallet Buying Guide"
4. "How to Choose a Leather Wallet"
5. "Leather Wallets: Complete Review"
[+ 10 more similar posts]
```

**Fix Applied:**
```
1. Merged all into: "Leather Wallets: Complete 2025 Guide"
   - Combined best content from all 15 posts
   - Added 2,000 words of new, unique content
   - Created comprehensive resource (buying guide + reviews + care tips)
   
2. Set up 301 redirects from 14 old posts to new comprehensive guide

3. Updated internal links throughout site

4. Promoted new comprehensive page
```

**Results (After 3 Months):**
```
- New guide ranks position #2 for "leather wallets"
- Traffic: 3,850 visits/month (up 1,032%)
- Also ranks for 76 related keywords
- Featured snippet captured for "how to care for leather wallet"
- Conversion rate: 5.4% (vs previous 1.8%)
```

## Summary: On-Page SEO Checklist

**Content Optimization:**
- [ ] High-quality, relevant content that answers user intent
- [ ] Appropriate length (1,500-2,500 words for competitive topics)
- [ ] Fresh, updated content with publication dates
- [ ] Proper formatting (headers, bullets, white space, images)
- [ ] 6th-8th grade reading level for general audiences
- [ ] E-E-A-T demonstrated (especially for YMYL content)
- [ ] Author credentials displayed
- [ ] Sources cited where appropriate

**Keyword Optimization:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in title tag (near beginning)
- [ ] Primary keyword in URL slug
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Keywords in subheadings (H2, H3)
- [ ] Keywords in image alt text (naturally)
- [ ] Natural keyword density (0.5-1.5%)
- [ ] LSI keywords throughout content
- [ ] Secondary keywords targeted in subheadings
- [ ] Long-tail variations included naturally
- [ ] No keyword cannibalization with other pages
