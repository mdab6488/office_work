
## Title Tag Length

### Optimal Length: 50-60 Characters

Google typically displays the first **50-60 characters** of a title tag. Mobile displays even less (~40-50 characters).

**Character Count Examples:**

**✅ Perfect Length (52 characters):**
```html
<title>Best Yoga Mats 2025 - Reviews & Buying Guide</title>
```
This displays fully in search results.

**⚠️ Too Long (78 characters):**
```html
<title>The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Best Yoga Mat for Your Practice in 2025</title>
```
Google will truncate this to: "The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Best Yoga Mat for Your Prac..."

---

### Real-World Examples

**Netflix (Perfect):**
```html
<title>Watch Movies & TV Shows Online | Netflix</title>
<!-- 44 characters - displays perfectly -->
```

**The New York Times (Good):**
```html
<title>The New York Times - Breaking News, US News, World News</title>
<!-- 60 characters - just at the limit -->
```

**Wikipedia (Too Long):**
```html
<title>Search engine optimization - Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</title>
<!-- 64 characters - gets cut off in mobile results -->
```

---

### Pixel Width Matters More Than Characters

Google measures title tags by **pixel width** (~600px desktop, ~500px mobile), not just character count.

**Why this matters:**
```html
<!-- These are both 40 characters, but different pixel widths -->

<title>WWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWWW</title>
<!-- Wide characters = truncated early -->

<title>iiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiii</title>
<!-- Narrow characters = displays more -->
```

**Pro Tip:** Uppercase letters and "W" characters take more space. "i" and "l" take less.
