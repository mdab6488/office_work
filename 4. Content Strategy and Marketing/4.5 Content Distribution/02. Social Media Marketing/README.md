
## Social Media Marketing

### What It Is
Social media marketing involves using social platforms to distribute content, engage with your audience, and build brand awareness. Each platform has unique characteristics and audience behaviors.

### Platform-Specific Strategies

#### 1. **LinkedIn** (B2B & Professional Content)

**Best For**: Industry insights, professional development, thought leadership

**Real-Life Example: HubSpot**
- Posts educational carousels about marketing strategies
- Shares blog articles with key takeaways in the post itself
- Uses employee advocacy (team members share company content)
- Result: 5+ million followers, high engagement on educational content

**Tactics**:
- Post native articles directly on LinkedIn
- Use carousel posts for step-by-step guides
- Engage in comments to boost visibility
- Best posting times: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM

#### 2. **Twitter/X** (Real-Time & News)

**Best For**: Quick updates, trending topics, customer service

**Real-Life Example: Wendy's**
- Uses humor and personality in tweets
- Engages directly with customers and competitors
- Creates viral threads about their products
- Result: 3.8+ million followers, massive brand awareness

**Tactics**:
- Thread longer content (8-15 tweets)
- Use visuals to increase engagement by 150%
- Post during peak hours: 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM
- Leverage trending hashtags strategically

**Example Thread**:
```
1/ I spent $10,000 testing Facebook ads. Here's what I learned:

2/ Most people waste money on the wrong objective. Use these instead...

[Continue with valuable insights]

15/ Hope this helps! Retweet if you found this valuable.
```

#### 3. **Instagram** (Visual Storytelling)

**Best For**: Behind-the-scenes, visual tutorials, brand personality

**Real-Life Example: Nike**
- Story highlights for product launches
- Reels showing athlete training routines
- User-generated content campaigns (#JustDoIt)
- Result: 300+ million followers, strong community engagement

**Tactics**:
- Reels for how-to content (highest reach)
- Stories for daily engagement and polls
- Grid posts for curated aesthetic
- Use 5-10 relevant hashtags (mix of popular and niche)

#### 4. **YouTube** (Long-Form Video)

**Best For**: Tutorials, product reviews, educational series

**Real-Life Example: Moz (Whiteboard Friday)**
- Weekly SEO education videos
- Consistent format and schedule
- Optimized titles and thumbnails
- Result: 250K+ subscribers, established authority in SEO

**Tactics**:
- Optimize titles for search (include keywords)
- Create compelling thumbnails (faces, text overlays)
- Post consistently (same day/time each week)
- Use end screens to promote other videos

#### 5. **TikTok** (Short-Form, Trend-Driven)

**Best For**: Educational entertainment, brand personality, viral trends

**Real-Life Example: Duolingo**
- Mascot-based content (Duo the Owl)
- Trends participation with educational twist
- Behind-the-scenes and humor
- Result: 10+ million followers, massive brand awareness among Gen Z

**Tactics**:
- Hook viewers in first 3 seconds
- Follow trending sounds and formats
- Post 1-3 times daily for algorithm favor
- Use on-screen text for accessibility

### Social Media Content Calendar Example

```
Monday: Blog post promotion (LinkedIn, Twitter)
Tuesday: Educational carousel (LinkedIn, Instagram)
Wednesday: Video tutorial (YouTube, TikTok)
Thursday: Industry news commentary (Twitter, LinkedIn)
Friday: Behind-the-scenes/culture (Instagram Stories, TikTok)
Weekend: User-generated content reshares
```
