
### Evergreen Content

**Definition:** Content that remains relevant, valuable, and searchable over time, continuing to drive traffic and engagement months or years after publication. It doesn't rely on current events or trends.

**Characteristics of Evergreen Content:**
- Timeless topics and information
- Continuously relevant to audience
- Requires minimal updates
- Generates consistent organic traffic
- Provides long-term ROI
- Focuses on fundamentals, not trends

**Benefits:**
- **Compound Traffic Growth:** Builds traffic over time
- **Cost-Effective:** One piece generates value for years
- **SEO Authority:** Demonstrates expertise on core topics
- **Consistent Leads:** Ongoing conversion opportunities
- **Foundation for Updates:** Easy to refresh and republish

**Evergreen vs. Timely Content:**

| Evergreen ✅ | Timely ❌ |
|-------------|-----------|
| "How to Tie a Tie" | "Top Tie Trends for Spring 2024" |
| "Beginner's Guide to Photography" | "Best Cameras Released This Month" |
| "How to Write a Resume" | "2024 Resume Statistics" |
| "Basic Yoga Poses" | "Why Yoga is Trending Right Now" |
| "Understanding Credit Scores" | "Fed Raises Interest Rates Again" |

**Types of Evergreen Content:**

**1. How-To Guides and Tutorials**
Step-by-step instructions for skills that don't change significantly

**Real-Life Example: WikiHow's Entire Business Model**
- 200,000+ how-to articles
- Topics: "How to Boil Water," "How to Tie Shoelaces"
- Simple, timeless skills
- Result: 100M+ monthly visitors, consistently in top 100 websites globally

**2. FAQs and Common Questions**
Answers to frequently asked questions in your industry

**Real-Life Example: Mayo Clinic's Health Q&A**
- Thousands of medical questions answered
- "What causes headaches?"
- "How much water should I drink?"
- "What is normal blood pressure?"
- Result: Millions of monthly visits, top health authority

**3. Glossary/Definition Content**
Explaining terminology and concepts

**Real-Life Example: Investopedia's Dictionary**
- 30,000+ financial term definitions
- "What is a 401k?"
- "Definition of Bull Market"
- "What is Compound Interest?"
- Result: 20M+ monthly visitors, #1 source for financial education

**4. List Posts of Timeless Value**
Lists that don't depend on current trends

**Examples:**
- "50 Ways to Save Money" (always relevant)
- "100 Creative Writing Prompts" (timeless)
- "25 Healthy Snack Ideas" (won't expire)
- "15 Study Techniques That Work" (fundamentals)

**5. "Ultimate Guide" Pillar Content**
Comprehensive resources on core topics

**Real-Life Example: HubSpot's Marketing Guides**
- "The Ultimate Guide to Email Marketing"
- "Complete Guide to Social Media"
- "Beginner's Guide to SEO"
- Updated annually but fundamentals remain
- Result: Hundreds of thousands of monthly visits per guide

**6. Best Practices and Principles**
Foundational advice in your field

**Examples:**
- "10 Principles of Good Design"
- "Customer Service Best Practices"
- "Time Management Fundamentals"
- "Email Etiquette Rules"

**7. Product Reviews (Carefully)**
Reviews can be evergreen if product is stable

**Real-Life Example: Wirecutter (NYT)**
- In-depth product reviews
- Focus on products with longevity
- "Best Coffee Maker" (category doesn't change much)
- Regular updates when needed
- Result: Sold to NYT for $30M

**Creating Evergreen Content:**

**Step 1: Identify Evergreen Topics**

Ask yourself:
- Will this be relevant in 2-5 years?
- Are people searching for this consistently?
- Is this a fundamental concept or skill?
- Does it answer a timeless question?

**Google Trends Test:**
- Search your topic in Google Trends
- Look for consistent search volume (not spiky)
- Evergreen topics show steady interest over time

**Step 2: Focus on Fundamentals**

Write about:
- Basic principles
- Core concepts
- Foundational skills
- How things work
- Problem-solving methods

**Step 3: Avoid Time-Sensitive Language**

❌ Avoid:
- "This year"
- "Currently"
- "Recently"
- "Latest"
- "Trending"
- "2024 update"
- "Right now"

✅ Use:
- "How to..."
- "What is..."
- "Ways to..."
- "Guide to..."
- "Basics of..."
- "Understanding..."

**Step 4: Include Timeless Examples**

- Use classic examples that won't become outdated
- Focus on principles, not specific tools (unless tool is industry standard)
- Explain concepts, not current events

**Maintaining Evergreen Content:**

**Quarterly Review Process:**
1. Check analytics for top-performing evergreen posts
2. Review for accuracy (are stats still current?)
3. Update examples if needed
4. Check for broken links
5. Refresh meta descriptions for SEO
6. Add new sections if topic has expanded

**Annual Deep Refresh:**
1. Rewrite outdated sections
2. Add new research or examples
3. Improve formatting and visuals
4. Update publication date
5. Re-promote as "Updated for [Year]"

**Promoting Evergreen Content:**

**1. Homepage/Website:**
- Feature in navigation
- "Popular Posts" sidebar
- "Start Here" page

**2. Email Campaigns:**
- Welcome sequence for new subscribers
- Resource round-ups
- Topic-specific drip campaigns

**3. Social Media:**
- Share multiple times (not just at publication)
- Reshare every 3-6 months
- Create new promotional angles

**4. Internal Linking:**
- Link from every relevant new post
- Update older posts with links to evergreen content
- Create topic clusters around evergreen pillars

**5. Paid Promotion:**
- Evergreen content justifies ad spend
- Long-term ROI on promotion
- Retargeting campaigns

**Metrics for Evergreen Content:**

Track these over time (months/years):
- **Consistent Traffic:** Should remain steady or grow
- **Time on Page:** Higher than average (people read thoroughly)
- **Backlinks:** Should accumulate over time
- **Rankings:** Should improve or maintain top positions
- **Conversions:** Ongoing lead generation
