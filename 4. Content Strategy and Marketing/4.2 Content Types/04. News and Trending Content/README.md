
### News and Trending Content

**Definition:** Time-sensitive content that capitalizes on current events, trending topics, industry news, or viral moments. The opposite of evergreen content, it's designed for immediate impact and short-term traffic spikes.

**Why Create Trending Content:**
- **Quick Traffic Wins:** Ride wave of existing interest
- **Social Media Virality:** Trending topics get more shares
- **Brand Relevance:** Show you're current and engaged
- **Newsjacking Opportunities:** Associate your brand with popular topics
- **Breaking Through Noise:** People actively searching for fresh takes
- **Link Earning:** News sites and blogs link to timely analysis

**When to Use Trending Content:**

✅ **Good Situations:**
- Major industry news (algorithm updates, regulations)
- Cultural moments relevant to your audience
- Viral trends with staying power (few days to weeks)
- Seasonal events (predictable)
- Breaking research or studies in your field

❌ **Avoid:**
- Controversial topics unrelated to brand
- Short-lived memes (hours of relevance)
- Tragedies or sensitive events (brand risk)
- Topics outside your expertise
- Bandwagoning without value-add

**Types of News and Trending Content:**

**1. News Commentary**
Your expert take on industry news

**Real-Life Example: Moz's Google Algorithm Update Posts**

Every time Google releases a major update:

**Formula:**
- **What Happened:** Summarize the update
- **What It Means:** Explain implications
- **What to Do:** Actionable advice

**Example: "Google's Core Update - March 2024"**
- Published within 24 hours of announcement
- Expert analysis from Moz team
- Specific recommendations for SEOs
- Result: Spike of 50,000+ visits in first week, establishes authority

**2. Trend Analysis**
Deep dive into emerging trends

**Real-Life Example: CB Insights' Trend Reports**

**Post: "The Rise of AI Agents in Enterprise Software"**
- Published as trend emerged
- Data-backed analysis
- Predictions for future
- Includes their proprietary research
- Result: Media coverage, backlinks from major publications

**3. Real-Time Marketing**
Immediate response to cultural moments

**Real-Life Example: Oreo's "Dunk in the Dark" Tweet**

**Context:** 2013 Super Bowl blackout (lights went out)

**Response:** Within minutes, Oreo tweeted:
"Power out? No problem. You can still dunk in the dark."
+ Simple image of Oreo in spotlight

**Result:**
- 15,000+ retweets in one hour
- Millions of impressions
- Featured in marketing textbooks
- Cost: Essentially zero
- Value: Massive brand awareness

**4. Newsjacking**
Injecting your brand into breaking news

**Real-Life Example: Dollar Shave Club and Gillette Recall**

When Gillette recalled razors:

**Dollar Shave Club's Response:**
- Twitter campaign: "No recalls here. Our blades are f***ing great."
- Blog post: "Why We'll Never Recall Our Razors"
- Explained their quality process
- Subtle jab at competitor

**Result:** Significant spike in subscriptions, media coverage

**5. Seasonal/Predictable Trends**
Content tied to annual events

**Real-Life Example: REI's #OptOutside Campaign**

**Launched:** Black Friday 2015

**Strategy:**
- Closed all stores on Black Friday
- Paid employees to spend day outside
- Encouraged customers to skip shopping
- Created movement against consumerism

**Annual Execution:**
- Blog content about outdoor activities
- Social media campaign
- User-generated content
- Partnership announcements

**Result:**
- 1.4B+ media impressions annually
- Strengthened brand identity
- Consistent PR win every year

**6. Hot Take / Contrarian Opinion**
Controversial stance on trending topic

**Real-Life Example: Basecamp's Stance on Work Culture**

When "hustle culture" was trending:

**Basecamp's Posts:**
- "Work Can Wait" blog post
- "40 Hours is Plenty"
- Critique of Silicon Valley work culture

**Why It Worked:**
- Contrarian to dominant narrative
- Aligned with founder's values
- Started conversation
- Generated debate (engagement)

**Result:** Viral spread, media coverage, positioned Basecamp as thought leader on work-life balance

**Creating Trending Content Efficiently:**

**1. Set Up Monitoring Systems**

**Tools:**
- **Google Trends:** Track rising searches
- **Twitter Trending:** Real-time topics
- **Google Alerts:** Keywords in your industry
- **Feedly:** RSS feeds from key sources
- **BuzzSumo:** Trending content in your niche
- **Reddit:** r/[your industry] for emerging topics

**2. Create Response Framework**

Have templates ready:

**Template: Industry News Commentary**
```
Title: [News Event] - What It Means for [Your Audience]

1. What Happened (100 words)
2. Context/Background (200 words)
3. Our Analysis (400 words)
4. What You Should Do (300 words)
5. Conclusion (100 words)
```

**Template: Hot Take**
```
Title: Why Everyone is Wrong About [Trending Topic]

1. Popular Opinion (150 words)
2. Why I Disagree (300 words)
3. Evidence/Examples (400 words)
4. What This Really Means (200 words)
5. Bottom Line (100 words)
```

**3. Speed Matters**

**Timeline for Trending Content:**
- **Breaking News:** Respond within 1-4 hours
- **Trending Topics:** Within 24 hours
- **Industry News:** Within 24-48 hours
- **Emerging Trends:** Within 1 week

**Real-Life Example: The Hustle's News Newsletter**

**Model:** Daily tech/business news with personality

**Process:**
- 5am: Scan overnight news
- 6am: Select top stories
- 6-8am: Write newsletter with commentary
- 8:30am: Send to 1.5M+ subscribers

**Formula:**
- News brief (50 words)
- The Hustle's take (100 words, humorous)
- Why it matters (50 words)

**Why It Works:**
- Timely (arrives in morning inbox)
- Voice/personality (not just news aggregation)
- Digestible format
- Consistent schedule

**Result:** Acquired by HubSpot for $27M

**Risks of Trending Content:**

**1. Insensitivity**
❌ **Example:** Brands tweeting jokes during tragedies

**Epic Fail:** Kenneth Cole during Cairo protests (2011)
- Tweet: "Millions are in uproar in #Cairo. Rumor is they heard our new spring collection is now available online..."
- Result: Massive backlash, apologies, brand damage

**2. Bandwagoning Without Value**
❌ **Example:** Forced connections to trends

**Cringe Example:** Brands trying to make every trend about their product
- "How to incorporate [unrelated product] into [viral dance trend]"

**3. Getting Facts Wrong**
- Trending content rushes can lead to errors
- Misinformation spreads quickly
- Damages credibility

**Best Practices:**

✅ **Do:**
- Verify facts before publishing
- Stay in your lane (relevant to brand)
- Add genuine value or insight
- Be authentic, not opportunistic
- Have approval process for sensitive topics

❌ **Don't:**
- Jump on every trend
- Fake enthusiasm for clicks
- Ignore brand voice for relevance
- Post without context
- Appropriate serious events for marketing

**Balancing Trending and Evergreen:**

**Recommended Mix:**
- **70% Evergreen:** Long-term traffic foundation
- **30% Trending:** Spikes, relevance, social shares

**Real-Life Example: BuzzFeed's Dual Strategy**

**Trending Side:**
- Daily celebrity news
- Viral quizzes ("Which Disney Princess Are You?")
- Trending topic listicles
- Reactions to cultural moments

**Evergreen Side:**
- "How to Clean Your Kitchen"
- Recipe collections
- Product round-ups
- Life hack guides

**Result:** Trending content brings initial traffic, evergreen content provides consistent baseline.

**Measuring Trending Content:**

**Short-Term Metrics (24-72 hours):**
- Traffic spike magnitude
- Social shares and engagement
- Comments and discussion
- Backlinks earned
- Media mentions

**Long-Term Value:**
- Did it build email list?
- Brand awareness bump?
- New audience reached?
- Strengthened positioning?
