
### Grammar and Spelling

Perfect grammar builds credibility. Errors undermine trust and harm SEO through reduced user engagement and sharing.

#### Why Grammar Matters for SEO

**Direct SEO Impacts:**
1. **User Experience Signals** - Errors cause bounces
2. **Credibility & Trust** - Affects link-building success
3. **Professional Image** - Influences brand searches
4. **Readability Scores** - Some algorithms consider this

**Real-Life Example - Study by Global Lingo:**

They found that:
- 74% of readers notice grammar errors
- 59% wouldn't use a company with obvious errors
- Spelling errors can reduce online sales by up to 50%

**Case Study:** When entrepreneur Charles Duncombe analyzed his website data, he found that a single spelling error cut sales in half. After fixing one error in a product description, revenue doubled.

#### Common Grammar Mistakes in SEO Content

**1. Your vs. You're**

❌ "Your going to love this product"
✅ "You're going to love this product"

**Memory trick:** You're = You are (if you can't substitute "you are," use "your")

---

**2. Its vs. It's**

❌ "The product has it's benefits"
✅ "The product has its benefits"

**Memory trick:** It's = It is/it has (only use with apostrophe if you can substitute these)

---

**3. There, Their, They're**

❌ "Their are many solutions"
❌ "There product is great"  
❌ "They're solutions are effective"

✅ "There are many solutions" (location/existence)
✅ "Their product is great" (possession)
✅ "They're effective" (they are)

---

**4. Affect vs. Effect**

❌ "This will effect your rankings"
❌ "The affect was immediate"

✅ "This will affect your rankings" (affect = verb = to influence)
✅ "The effect was immediate" (effect = noun = the result)

**Memory trick:** Affect is an Action (both start with A)

---

**5. Lose vs. Loose**

❌ "Don't loose your rankings"
❌ "These pants are too lose"

✅ "Don't lose your rankings"
✅ "These pants are too loose"

---

**6. Fewer vs. Less**

❌ "Less people are shopping in stores"
❌ "You'll make less mistakes"

✅ "Fewer people are shopping in stores" (can count people)
✅ "You'll make fewer mistakes" (can count mistakes)
✅ "Less time" (can't count time)
✅ "Less water" (can't count water)

**Rule:** Use "fewer" for countable nouns, "less" for uncountable nouns

---

**7. That vs. Which**

❌ "The strategy which worked best was email marketing"
✅ "The strategy that worked best was email marketing"

❌ "Our product, that comes in three colors, is popular"
✅ "Our product, which comes in three colors, is popular"

**Rule:**
- "That" introduces essential information (no commas)
- "Which" introduces non-essential information (use commas)

---

**8. Comma Splices**

❌ "Content marketing is effective, it generates leads"
✅ "Content marketing is effective. It generates leads"
✅ "Content marketing is effective; it generates leads"
✅ "Content marketing is effective, and it generates leads"

**Rule:** Can't join independent clauses with just a comma

---

**9. Subject-Verb Agreement**

❌ "The team are working on it"
❌ "Each of the strategies have benefits"

✅ "The team is working on it" (team = singular)
✅ "Each of the strategies has benefits" (each = singular)

---

**10. Dangling Modifiers**

❌ "Running quickly, the finish line was crossed"
(The finish line wasn't running!)

✅ "Running quickly, the athlete crossed the finish line"

#### Style Guide Decisions

Every brand needs consistency. Here's what to document:

**Real-Life Example - The Associated Press (AP) Stylebook:**

```markdown
## Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Use figures for 10 and above
- Exception: Percentages always use figures (5%)

## Company Names
- Always use official spelling: eBay, iPhone, McDonald's
- First reference: Full company name
- Subsequent: Shortened version OK

## Dates and Times
- March 15, 2024 (not 15th March)
- 9 a.m., 5:30 p.m. (lowercase, with periods)

## Titles
- Capitalize before names: President John Smith
- Lowercase after names: John Smith, president of Acme Corp.
```

---

**Real-Life Example - Shopify's Internal Style Guide:**

```markdown
## Voice Decisions

### Contractions: YES
✅ "You're building something amazing"
❌ "You are building something amazing"

### Oxford Comma: YES
✅ "Sell products, services, and subscriptions"
❌ "Sell products, services and subscriptions"

### Exclamation Points: Sparingly
✅ "Your store is live!" (celebrations only)
❌ "Check out these features!"

### Numbers in Headlines
✅ "7 Ways to Increase Sales"
❌ "Seven Ways to Increase Sales"

### Product Names
✅ "Shopify Plus" (not "shopify plus" or "SHOPIFY PLUS")
✅ "Shopify Payments" (always capitalized)
```

#### Common Style Decisions to Document

**1. Serial (Oxford) Comma**

Choose one:
- "red, white, and blue" (with Oxford comma)
- "red, white and blue" (without Oxford comma)

**Real-Life Example:** Apple uses Oxford comma. BuzzFeed doesn't.

**2. Capitalization**

Document your rules:
```markdown
## Headline Capitalization
We use title case (capitalize major words):
✅ "How to Build a Successful Online Store"

Not sentence case:
❌ "How to build a successful online store"

## Feature Names
Always capitalize:
- Shopify Payments
- Buy Button
- Point of Sale

Never capitalize:
- online store
- email marketing
- product page
```

**3. Number Style**

```markdown
## When to Use Numerals

Always use numerals for:
- Percentages: 5%, 99%
- Ages: 5 years old, 99 years old
- Measurements: 5 feet, 99 pounds
- Money: $5, $99
- Dates: March 5, May 99th

Spell out:
- Numbers one through nine in body text
- Numbers that start sentences
- Approximate numbers: "a few thousand people"
```

**4. Acronyms and Abbreviations**

```markdown
## First Reference
Spell out with acronym in parentheses:
"Content Management System (CMS)"

## Subsequent References
Use acronym: "The CMS allows..."

## Well-Known Acronyms (No need to spell out)
- SEO
- CEO
- USA
- FAQ
- ATM
```

#### Industry-Specific Grammar Notes

**Legal Content:**
- Extra precision required
- "May" vs. "might" vs. "can" vs. "shall" have specific meanings
- Passive voice sometimes necessary ("Claims must be filed...")
- Avoid contractions in formal legal text

**Medical Content:**
- Use patient-first language: "people with diabetes" not "diabetics"
- Be precise with terminology
- Avoid metaphors that might confuse
- Define medical terms on first use

**Technical Content:**
- Consistent code formatting (inline `code` vs. code blocks)
- Precise terminology (don't interchange "method" and "function" randomly)
- Clear action words ("click," "tap," "select")
