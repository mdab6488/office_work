**What It Is:**
Getting your business featured in local news outlets, blogs, and media publications to earn authoritative links and brand exposure.

**Why It Matters:**
- News sites have high domain authority (DA 60-90)
- Editorial links are most valuable for SEO
- Builds brand credibility and trust
- Creates shareable content

**Real-Life Examples:**

**Example 1: Eco-Friendly Cleaning Service - Earth Sweep**
**PR Campaign:** "Local Business Eliminates Plastic Bottles"

Action Taken:
- Switched to reusable bottles, saving 10,000 plastic bottles/year
- Wrote press release highlighting environmental impact
- Sent to local news outlets with photos

Results:
- Featured in city newspaper (DA 72) with dofollow link
- Picked up by regional news site
- Shared by environmental blog (DA 45)
- Total: 4 high-authority links
- Traffic spike: 300% increase that week
- 25 new customers mentioned seeing the article

**Example 2: Bakery Launches "Pay What You Can" Day**
**PR Campaign:** Community Support Initiative

Action Taken:
- Held monthly "Pay What You Can" day for struggling families
- Invited local news to cover first event
- Shared customer stories (with permission)

Results:
- TV news segment with website mention
- Newspaper article with link
- Featured on 3 local blogs
- Became permanent monthly coverage opportunity

**Example 3: Gym Owner's Transformation Story**
**PR Campaign:** Personal Success Story

Action Taken:
- Owner lost 150 lbs using own gym programs
- Pitched story to local magazine with before/after photos
- Tied to gym's new affordable membership program

Results:
- 3-page magazine feature with website and social links
- Magazine story republished online (permanent link)
- Booked for local radio interview
- Membership increased 40% that month

**Types of Newsworthy Stories:**

1. **Community Impact:**
   - Charity work or donations
   - Job creation or hiring initiatives
   - Free services for community
   - Environmental initiatives

2. **Milestone Achievements:**
   - Business anniversaries (10, 25, 50 years)
   - Expansion or new locations
   - Major awards or recognition
   - Revenue milestones

3. **Unique Angle:**
   - Unusual services or products
   - Interesting founder story
   - Industry innovation
   - Local first or only

4. **Trend Tying:**
   - Connect your business to current trends
   - Provide expert commentary
   - Release relevant data or survey results

5. **Human Interest:**
   - Employee success stories
   - Customer transformation stories
   - Overcoming adversity
   - Family business legacy

**Implementation Steps:**

**Step 1: Build Media List**
```
Local outlets to target:
- City/regional newspapers
- TV news stations (website coverage)
- Local magazines
- Community newsletters
- Neighborhood blogs
- Local podcast shows
- Chamber of Commerce publications
- University publications (if nearby)
```

**Step 2: Craft Your Story**
Use this formula:
- **Hook:** Attention-grabbing opening
- **Why Now:** Timely relevance
- **Local Angle:** Why it matters to the community
- **Quotes:** From you, customers, or beneficiaries
- **Call to Action:** What readers should do
- **Contact Info:** How to reach you

**Best Practices:**
- Research journalist's beat before pitching
- Send pitches Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm
- Personalize each pitch (no mass emails)
- Include high-quality photos
- Make it easy (provide quotes, facts, angles)
- Follow up once after 3-4 days

**Step 4: Maximize Coverage**

When you get coverage:
1. Share on all social media platforms
2. Embed article link on your website
3. Include in email newsletter
4. Frame physical copy in office
5. Add "As Featured In" logos to website
6. Use in future marketing materials
7. Thank the journalist publicly

**HARO (Help A Reporter Out):**

Free service where journalists request expert sources.

**How to Use:**
1. Sign up at helpareporter.com
2. Choose relevant industries
3. Receive 3 daily emails with queries
4. Respond quickly (within 2-4 hours)
5. Provide valuable, quotable responses

**HARO Success Example:**
```
Query: "Looking for local business owners who pivoted during COVID"

Response from salon owner:
- Detailed story of launching virtual hair color consultations
- Specific metrics: 200 virtual appointments, $15K revenue
- Client success stories
- High-quality headshot attached

Result: 
- Featured in regional business journal (DA 68)
- Article linked to salon website
- 3 other media outlets picked up story
- Total: 4 high-authority backlinks
```
