
## Advanced Workflows

### Workflow 1: Weekly Competitive Monitoring

**Automated Process**:

```
Every Monday Morning:

Step 1: Check Key Rankings (15 min)
Search main keywords in Google
Note SEOquake metrics for top 10
Save to tracking spreadsheet

Keywords to track:
- "your main keyword"
- "your product keyword"
- "your service keyword"
- "your location keyword"

Step 2: Spot Changes (10 min)
Compare to last week:
- New competitors appearing?
- Position movements?
- Backlink changes?
- Traffic changes (SEMrush rank)?

Step 3: Quick Competitor Check (20 min)
Visit top 3 competitors
SEOquake diagnosis:
- New content published?
- Site changes?
- New backlinks?
- Social activity?

Step 4: Document & Plan (15 min)
Note in tracking sheet:
- Significant changes
- Threats (competitors improving)
- Opportunities (competitors declining)
- Action items for this week

Total: 1 hour per week
Result: Always aware of competitive landscape
```

### Workflow 2: Content Optimization Pipeline

**Monthly Process**:

```
Goal: Systematically improve underperforming content

Step 1: Identify Low-Performers (30 min)
Export all blog post URLs
Check each with SEOquake:
- Low backlinks
- Low social shares
- Poor rankings
- Thin content

Create priority list:
High potential = Good traffic but low conversions
Quick wins = Simple fixes needed
Major overhaul = Complete rewrite needed

Step 2: Competitive Analysis (45 min per post)
For each post to optimize:

Search target keyword
Analyze top 5 with SEOquake:
- Average word count
- Average backlinks
- Average social shares
- Common content elements

Your post: "Email Marketing Tips" (800 words)
Top 5 average: 2,400 words
Action: Expand significantly

Step 3: Optimization (Varies)
For each post:
- Run SEOquake diagnosis
- Fix technical issues
- Expand content
- Improve internal linking
- Add/update images with alt text
- Enhance meta descriptions
- Update for current year

Step 4: Promotion (1 hour per post)
After update:
- Share on social media
- Email to list
- Reach out for backlinks
- Submit to relevant communities
- Update internal links from other posts

Step 5: Track Results (15 min per post monthly)
Monitor with SEOquake:
- Ranking improvements
- Backlink growth
- Social share increases
- Traffic changes (SEMrush rank)

Target: Optimize 4-8 posts per month
Result: Continuous content improvement
```

### Workflow 3: New Content Launch Checklist

**Every New Post**:

```
Pre-Publish SEOquake Checks:

□ Run Diagnosis
  □ Single H1 tag present
  □ Meta description (150-160 chars)
  □ Title tag (50-60 chars)
  □ All images have alt text
  □ At least 5 internal links
  □ 2-3 external authority links
  □ Proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3)
  □ Mobile-friendly
  □ HTTPS enabled

□ Keyword Density Check
  □ Primary keyword: 1-2%
  □ Related keywords present
  □ Natural language (not stuffed)
  □ LSI keywords included

□ Competitive Comparison
  □ Search target keyword
  □ Compare to top 5:
    □ Word count competitive?
    □ Content depth adequate?
    □ Visual content included?
    □ Unique angle present?

□ Internal Link Optimization
  □ Linked from relevant posts
  □ Links to relevant posts
  □ Clear anchor text
  □ Topic cluster connected

Post-Publish (Week 1):

□ Monitor with SEOquake
  □ Check initial indexing
  □ Monitor for backlinks
  □ Track social shares
  □ Note ranking position

□ Promotion completed
  □ Shared on social media
  □ Emailed to list
  □ Submitted to communities
  □ Outreach to influencers

Post-Publish (Week 4):

□ First month review
  □ Ranking check
  □ Backlink count
  □ Social engagement
  □ Identify improvements

Result: Consistent high-quality launches
```

### Workflow 4: SERP Feature Targeting

**Strategy**: Optimize for featured snippets, People Also Ask, etc.

```
Step 1: Identify Opportunities (30 min weekly)

Search your target keywords
SEOquake overlay shows rankings
Note SERP features present:
- Featured snippets
- People Also Ask
- Image pack
- Video carousel
- Local pack

Check if competitors own these:
Example:
Keyword: "how to make cold brew"
- Featured snippet: competitor-blog.com (not you)
- PAA: 4 questions shown
- Image pack: Showing competitor images

Step 2: Analyze Featured Content

Visit featured snippet page:
Run SEOquake diagnosis:
- How is content structured?
- What format (list, table, paragraph)?
- How long is the answer?
- What's the page authority?

Example snippet analysis:
- Format: Numbered list
- Length: 6 steps, ~150 words
- Authority: 234 backlinks
- Structure: H2 question, followed by list

Your content:
- Format: Long paragraph
- Length: ~500 words, buried in text
- Authority: 45 backlinks
- Structure: No clear question format

Step 3: Optimize Your Content

Create snippet-friendly format:
- Add clear H2 with exact question
- Provide concise answer immediately
- Use appropriate format (list, table, etc.)
- Keep answer 40-60 words for paragraph snippets
- Use proper markup (lists, tables)

Before:
"Cold brew is great... [long intro] ...here's how you make it..."

After:
## How to Make Cold Brew Coffee at Home

To make cold brew:
1. Coarse grind coffee beans
2. Mix 1:4 ratio coffee to cold water
3. Steep 12-24 hours in fridge
4. Strain through fine mesh
5. Dilute with water or milk
6. Serve over ice

[Then detailed explanations below]

Step 4: Schema Markup

Add HowTo schema:
```
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "HowTo",
  "name": "How to Make Cold Brew Coffee",
  "step": [
    {
      "@type": "HowToStep",
      "text": "Coarse grind coffee beans"
    },
    // ... more steps
  ]
}
```
```

Step 5: Monitor Results

Check weekly with SEOquake:
- Featured snippet obtained?
- PAA appearance?
- Image pack inclusion?
- Traffic impact?

Result: Increased visibility and CTR
```
